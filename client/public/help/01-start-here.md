# What is NoticeBoard?

⏱️ Reading time: 2 minutes

## The big idea

NoticeBoard puts useful information on TVs and tablets around your workplace. Things like:

- Today's production numbers
- Safety messages
- Birthdays and announcements
- Stock levels
- Anything you'd normally print and stick on a wall

You change it from your computer, and the screens update on their own. **You never have to walk over to the TV.**

## How it fits together

```
You → NoticeBoard (on your computer)
              ↓
        Saves your change
              ↓
   TVs and tablets show it
```

Three big pieces to know:

### 1. Boards 📺
A **board** is one screen of information. Each TV in your building shows one board. You can have lots of boards (one for the canteen, one for the warehouse, one for reception…).

### 2. Pages 📑
A board can be one page, or it can flip between several pages — like slides in PowerPoint. Set a page to show for 10 seconds, and after 10 seconds it goes to the next one.

### 3. Widgets 🧩
A page is made up of **widgets**. A widget is one box that shows one thing:

- 📋 **Table** — rows and columns of numbers
- 📊 **Chart** — bars or pie chart
- 🖼️ **Image** — a picture, logo, or photo
- 📄 **Template** — a ready-made design you fill in
- 📌 **Notice** — a message from your team

## Two ways your data gets in

When your numbers change (production count, sales, stock…), you have **two choices**:

### Option A: Type them in 📝
Open the board, click the widget, change the numbers, click **Save**. Done.

### Option B: Connect to a data source 🔗
You set this up once. After that, NoticeBoard pulls the numbers automatically:

- **URL** — from another system that has an API (your factory dashboard, weather service, etc.)
- **JSON file** — a file someone exports from their system. You upload it when the numbers change.

Most non-technical staff use **Option A** for things that change all the time and **Option B** for things that someone else gives them in a file.

## What you'll do most often

Honestly? Three things, over and over:

1. **Open a board → change a few values → save.**
2. **Upload a new file when one arrives.**
3. **Once in a while, change the logo, colours, or message text.**

That's it. The rest of this help guide shows you each of those, step by step.
